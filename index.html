Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to my idea after I submit it?

Your idea is reviewed by our core team and, if valid, added to our private, internal Idea Bank. This bank is the *only* source we use to create future implementation programmes, such as startup cohorts and policy-change drives.

Will I get paid? Will my idea be stolen?

This programme is *not* for buying ideas. It's for collecting them to build community projects. You will not be paid for the submission.

Regarding IP, our promise is to always provide **recognition** to the original submitter (unless you choose to be anonymous). By submitting, you grant us a license to develop the idea. The specific IP terms will be determined by the future implementation programme (e.g., a startup cohort will have different terms than a community project).

Who can submit an idea?

Everyone! Students, professionals, developers, academics, and any concerned citizen. We believe good ideas can come from anywhere.

What kind of ideas are you looking for?

We're looking for any idea—big or small—that can create positive social change. This could be a concept for a new app, a piece of hardware, a community-run service, or a change to a local or public policy. If it's a solution to a real problem, we want to hear it.
